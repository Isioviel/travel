Day Fifteen: Kyoto

Thursday 18th May 2023

Uji (tea day!)

What We Had Planned

  • The bike tour guides drop us off at the train station, so when we arrive:
    • Put our bags in a locker.
    • Buy tickets for the Sagano Scenic Railway at Arashiyama for Monday morning.
    • Get on the train (Nara Line, about 30 minutes) to Uji for a relaxed day of tea drinking.

  • Check out a few tea places to see if they are going to need reservations. If so, book and plan timings occordingly:
    • Nakamura T≈çkichi - they have amazing looking tea-flavoured desserts. It's apparently social-media-famous so might be busy.
    • Mitsuboshien Kanbayashi Sannyu - they may do same-day tea ceremony reservations.
    • Taihoan - cheaply priced tea ceremony (it's run by the city), but cannot tell if they have re-opened since Covid.
    • Fukujuen Uji tea factory - run various tea workshops but ask for in-person reservations 3-days in advance. So pop in and see what's running and if they have free slots but don't expect anything.

  • Spend time around these reservations wandering and drinking tea.
  • Non-tea:
    • Visit Byodoin (devilfart!) temple.
    • Uji Shrine, rabbit-themed.
    • Daikichiyama Observation Deck.
    • Tale of Genji Museum.
    • Wandering by the river.
  • Tea:
    • Tsuen Teahouse and shop, oldest teahouse in the world.
    • Takumi-no Yakata, good at explaining how to brew the different teas.
