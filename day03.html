Day Three: Tokyo

Saturday 6th May 2023

Imperial Palace & Science

What We Had Planned

Note: I had intended to sign us up for a guided tour of the Imperial Palace grounds, but mistook the month that booking opened (1st April not 1st May!), and slots were all gone when I realised a couple of days later. We could arrive early and try to get walk-in tickets, but instead we'll have a leisurely breakfast.

Imperial Palace

  • Walk or metro in the right direction, stopping for a nice breakfast somewhere on the way.
  • Wander the Imperial Palace gardens, including the ruins of Edo castle - I downloaded an audio guide tour, so we can take headphones with us and learn some history.
  • Tea break!
  • Head to Tsukiji Outer Market before it closes at 2pm, to buy tea from Maruyama's Seaweed and maybe get snacks.
  • Visit Tsukiji Hongwanji temple.
  • Walk back to the apartment via more bridges and a small shrine.
  • Convenience store lunch (if we haven't already eaten), then see how tired we are (expecting jetlag to catch up with us if it hasn't already!).
  • If we still have energy, proceed to part two, otherwise skip it and relax.

Science & Tech

  • Metro to Odaiba.
  • See the Unicorn Gundam statue (it moves at 5pm).
  • Various science/tech places, some free, no idea how much time they will take so just do what we fancy:
    • Kawasaki Robostage.
    • National Museum of Emerging Science and Innovation
    • Panasonic Center.
  • Walk over the Rainbow Bridge at/after sundown, it lights up.
  • Eat on the other side of the bridge, then metro back.
