Day Two: Tokyo

Friday 5th May 2023

Asakusa & Akihabara

What We Had Planned

Asakusa

  • Walk to Nihombashi Station, then take the Asakusa to Asakusa Station - this will take about 30 minutes.
  • Join walking tour of Asakusa at 10am (2h) to see all the main sights (without needing to navigate ourselves, as we'll probably be jetlagged):
    • Kaminari-mon gate of the temple.
    • Tokyo Skytree (not to go up it).
    • Asahi building 'golden turd'.
    • Five Stories Pagoda (at the shrine).
    • Nakamise Shopping Street.
    • Denboin Street.
    • Sensō-ji temple.
  • Lunch near the station, and matcha ice cream at Suzukien.
  • Look for secondhand yukata at Tatsumi.
  • Tea ceremony class at Shizu-Kokoro booked in for 2pm.
  • Maybe go for a drink at Sumidagawa Brewing (craft beer pub) attached to the Asahi building.
  • We can head back to the apartment for a couple of hours rest if we need it, as things in Akihabara are open late.

Akihabara

  • Head to Akihabara either via metro or walking along the river path.
  • Might be time to visit Origami Kaikan, with a gallery and demonstrations (it closes at 16:30 though, so this is unlikely).
  • Nearby (heading back towards Akihabara station) is a Confucian temple, Yushima Seido.
  • Wander around, look in the gaming/electronics shops, and play in the arcades.
  • Eat here somewhere, or go back to the apartment if we're too tired to stay out.
  • Maybe go for a drink at Game Bar A-Button, a bar with retro games.
  • To return, we can get the Hibiya Line from Akihabara to Kayabachō Station, or it's about a 40 minute walk back.
